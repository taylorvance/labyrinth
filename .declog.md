## 2026-07-16 Arcade Key-Gate Loop

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: core-loop, ai, visuals
- Refs: SPEC.md, README.md
- Decision: Pivot the playable prototype from open-exit head-start escape to a full-visibility arcade room loop with an entrance gate, one key, one locked exit gate on another wall, loopier mazes, and readable hunter states. The hunter breaks through the entrance gate after the countdown, leaving that doorway blocked.
- Rationale: The open-exit version collapsed into sprinting toward the finish. A gate-to-gate room structure supports eventual multi-room progression, and a visible key-gate objective forces crossing and rerouting while preserving the arcade maze feel.
- Alternatives: Prep/discovery phase with hidden map information; trap-focused preparation; full rewrite with Pac-Man assets.

## 2026-07-16 Endless Rooms And Bounded Assist

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: core-loop, controls, ai
- Refs: SPEC.md, README.md
- Decision: Make reaching the exit advance directly into the next room with a short transition, add limited tap-to-path movement for nearby destinations, and make the hunter periodically reacquire the player's current position when it lacks clues.
- Rationale: Persistent win overlays interrupt the intended room chain, mobile arrows are too demanding as the primary control surface, and the hunter's non-omniscient behavior needs a bounded competence boost before deeper AI work.
- Alternatives: Keep one-room win/loss flow; restore fully omniscient pathing; implement full tap-to-anywhere maze pathfinding.

## 2026-07-16 Playability Diagnostics Filter

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: tuning, procedural-generation, ai
- Refs: src/game.ts, src/tuning.ts, SPEC.md
- Decision: Add route/timing diagnostics and use them to skip generated rooms that are too short, too long, under-supported by head start, or too tight under diagnostic hunter arrival timing.
- Rationale: Playtests showed the hunter/head-start tuning could feel unfair before the player had meaningful choices. A lightweight filter catches mathematically bad rooms without pretending to measure fun.
- Alternatives: Pure manual tuning; full game simulation; visible debug dashboard.

## 2026-07-16 Gate Position Continuity

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: room-transition, procedural-generation
- Refs: src/game.ts, src/App.tsx
- Decision: Carry the previous room's exit gate row/column into the next generated room as an entrance on the opposite wall, carving a short connection if that exact interior cell is not already connected.
- Rationale: Gate-to-gate progression should read as spatial continuity across rooms, not as a random respawn after each transition.
- Alternatives: Random entrance per room; choose only naturally connected matching entrances and reject more seeds; represent continuity visually without enforcing it in generation.

## 2026-07-16 Sight-Limited Hunter Pursuit

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: ai, tuning, agency
- Refs: src/game.ts, src/App.tsx, src/tuning.ts
- Decision: Make the hunter sprint only while it has direct line of sight or while executing a sight-derived corridor pursuit target. When sight is lost, it runs through the last seen cell to the next fork/dead end, then drops into slower tracking/search. Sprinting accelerates only after a longer straightaway and loses speed at turns. Proximity and scent clues create slow tracking targets, not sprint-speed omniscience. The room filter uses a blended 180ms diagnostic pursuit pace rather than the 125ms sprint, because full-route sprint timing would model an omniscient hunter the runtime no longer has.
- Rationale: The player needs agency around corners, loops, and forks. This keeps the hunter competent and readable without letting it continuously know the player's exact position.
- Alternatives: Fully omniscient fast pursuit; simple sprint to exact last-known position only; purely random search after sight loss.

## 2026-07-16 Continuous Player Cornering

- Status: Active experiment
- Topics: controls, movement, simulation
- Refs: src/App.tsx, src/tuning.ts
- Decision: Replace tile-step player coyote and hairpin heuristics with continuous tile-space player movement, a desired direction, a geometric cornering window around tile centers, and centerline correction while turning.
- Rationale: Pac-Man-like control feel comes from pre/post cornering in a continuous movement model, not from accumulating special cases on discrete tile steps. This keeps browser runtime cheap and makes future deterministic simulation simpler.
- Alternatives: Keep tile-step movement with buffered input; add more coyote/hairpin heuristics; implement full pixel-perfect Pac-Man movement.
